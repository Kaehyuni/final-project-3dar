Biology Made Visual

Hi, I’m Kenny Nguyen, a Biology Tutor & Academic Coach at Dallas College Richland Campus Learning Commons. BioBridge uses visual learning and augmented reality to help students truly understand biology not only just memorize it.

About BioBridge

Many students struggle with biology because it is often taught through dense textbooks and static diagrams. BioBridge was created to change that experience.

As a Biology Tutor and Academic Coach, I work one-on-one with students to break down complex topics like cell structure, membrane transport, and protein synthesis using visual explanations and interactive tools. I can see how it is atmost difficult for student to engage and learn when they are glued to long straining of information all at once. Especially when it comes exploring information from the textbooks and lecture powerpoint. It is harder for them to digest information. To make it easier for them, I want to share my experience when it comes to finding resources, a 3D augmented reality where they can interact, and amazing resources such as visual representation in videos. I beleive that education can be represented as something interactive. Especially when it stucked with me when I was student. I hope my animations and 3D modeling can help bring a brigde to education in Biology.

My Inspiration

My passion for visual learning began in 7th grade with my science teacher, Mr. Andy Thomason. He introduced our class to augmented reality as a way to explore scientific concepts beyond the textbook.

“Seeing biology through AR made makes the representation feel way more interactive. We weren’t just reading about cells we were exploring them.”

That experience shaped how I support students today. BioBridge continues that same philosophy: learning is most effective when students can see, explore, and interact with what they study.
